<p><p>Sodium soap-based dry wire drawing lubricants are crucial for reducing friction and wear during the wire drawing process. The two primary market types are Sodium Hydroxide and Sodium Stearate. Sodium Hydroxide acts as a strong alkaline agent, facilitating improved lubrication properties and enhancing the drawing process. In contrast, Sodium Stearate serves as a fatty acid soap, offering superior adhesion and film formation on wire surfaces. Both contribute to enhanced surface finish and overall efficiency in wire production.</p></p>
