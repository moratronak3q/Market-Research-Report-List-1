<p><p>The natural food colorant market encompasses various types, including caramel color, lutein, capsanthin, and anthocyanins, each derived from natural sources. Caramel color, produced by heating sugars, adds brown hues to food. Lutein, found in leafy greens, imparts a yellow color. Capsanthin, extracted from red peppers, provides a vibrant red hue, while anthocyanins, sourced from fruits like blueberries and blackberries, offer a range of red, purple, and blue shades. These colorants are favored for their visual appeal and health benefits.</p></p>
