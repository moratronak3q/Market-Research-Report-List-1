<p><ul><li>Disperse Dyes</li><li>Reactive Dyes</li><li>Sulfur Dyes</li><li>Vat Dyes</li><li>Acid Dyes</li><li>Other Dyes</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The dyestuff market consists of various types, each suited for specific applications. Disperse dyes are used primarily for synthetic fibers. Reactive dyes form covalent bonds with fibers, providing vivid colors and durability, ideal for cotton. Sulfur dyes offer cost-effective coloring for textiles, particularly denim. Vat dyes are known for their excellent lightfastness, suitable for cotton and cellulose fibers. Acid dyes work best on protein-based fibers like wool and silk, while other dyes cater to niche markets with varied functionalities.</p></p>
