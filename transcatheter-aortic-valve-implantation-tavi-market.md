<p><p>Transcatheter Aortic Valve Implantation (TAVI) is a minimally invasive procedure used to treat aortic valve stenosis by replacing the diseased valve with a new one. The primary approaches for TAVI include the transfemoral approach, where access is gained through the femoral artery, and the transapical approach, which involves accessing the heart through an incision in the chest wall. Other methods may include subclavian or direct aortic access. Each approach offers unique advantages depending on the patient's anatomy and clinical conditions.</p></p>
