<p><p>The consumer foam market comprises two primary types: flexible foam and rigid foam. Flexible foam, often made from polyurethane, is widely used in applications such as mattresses, cushions, and upholstery due to its ability to compress and return to shape. Rigid foam, on the other hand, is denser and used for insulation in construction, packaging, and automotive applications. Both types serve distinct consumer needs, with flexible foam focusing on comfort and support, while rigid foam emphasizes durability and thermal efficiency.</p></p>
