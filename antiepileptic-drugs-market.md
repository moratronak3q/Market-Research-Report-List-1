<p><p>The antiepileptic drugs market can be divided into two primary types based on the classification of epilepsy: partial and generalized. Partial epilepsy drugs target seizures that originate in a specific area of the brain, often affecting one hemisphere. Generalized epilepsy drugs address seizures that impact both hemispheres simultaneously, resulting in a broader range of symptoms. This segmentation allows for tailored treatment options, enhancing efficacy and patient outcomes in managing different epilepsy forms across diverse populations.</p></p>
