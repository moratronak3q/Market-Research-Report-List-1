<p><p>The near-beer market encompasses beverages with very low alcohol content, primarily focusing on two production methods: limit fermentation and dealcoholization. Limit fermentation involves restricting the fermentation process to produce beers with less than 0.5% alcohol. In contrast, the dealcoholization method starts with regular beer that has alcohol removed through various techniques, such as evaporation or filtration. Both methods cater to consumers seeking the taste of beer without the intoxicating effects, appealing to health-conscious individuals and those adhering to strict alcohol regulations.</p></p>
